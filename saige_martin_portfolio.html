I. On the work
A few principles, plainly.

A working thesis.

§Animals first.

§Records second.

§The herd is a long sentence.

§You finish the chores.

Livestock work has a quality most jobs don't — it doesn't stop because you're tired, or because the weather turned, or because the calendar says it's a holiday. The cattle eat at the same time every day for the rest of your life.

That cadence is the thing I've come to like most about the work. It's also the discipline that quietly underwrites everything else I do — the photography, the records, the academic work. If you can run a barn, you can run almost anything.

What I aim for in any operation is the same: clean records, well-cared-for animals, and the kind of preparation that makes a show ring or a sale day feel inevitable rather than improvised.
